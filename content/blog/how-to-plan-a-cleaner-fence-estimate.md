A better estimate starts with better context.

Before the first meeting, it helps to know the rough line of the fence, the material direction you are considering, the access points that matter, and whether the project also includes gates or adjacent exterior work.

Photos, measurements, and a short list of priorities make the estimate more useful. That allows the conversation to move beyond price alone and toward the right scope.

The goal is not to over-prepare. The goal is to make the first review precise enough that the project can move faster with fewer assumptions.
